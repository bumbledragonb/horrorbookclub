Welcome to our Horror Bookclub!

Give me horror books or give me death!

We love to read, but most importantly we want to be terrified.

If you, like us, have been chasing those knots in your stomach and cold shivers since the first time you were alone in the dark, the first campfire story you heard or the first horror book you picked up, be it Goosebumps or Scary Stories to Tell In The Dark, you’ve come to the right place. Beware though, because once you dive deep, you might never emerge from these abyssal depths. Once you know something, you can never unknow it and you might wish an author didn’t give shape to your new worst nightmare or anxiety you never thought about before. But, don’t panic (yet), we are here for you, gathering around our glowing screens for comfort, security and company, much like our ancestors did around the campfire.

Book of the Month

Every month we chose a horror book to read and at the end of the month, we schedule virtual meetings to talk about the book and anything else that might be related to it. Throughout the month, our members are active on our Discord. We discuss all kinds of topics ranging from music, art, movies, pop culture, true crime, biology, cake recipes to a remote community in the Appalachian Mountains or a language you never heard of before today. We love to pool our knowledge and our opinions, and we are a very welcoming and diverse community. We believe this enriches our reading experiences.

Our next month’s read is chosen by a poll at the end of each month and members are free to campaign for an author or advocate a particular book choice, as long as they don’t spam it. We read everything from obscure classics to new releases and we particularly focus on books that do something different and conjure fresh new horrors.

A community of readers

We are all horror lovers here and that bind us together, but most of all, we love talking about books. The books we read, the books we want to read, the books we hated, the books we loved and pretty much everything related to books and, from our experience, books are related to pretty much everything. Join our community not just to discuss our book of the month, but also to be part of a community of readers that are always open to give and receive recommendations, discuss, and learn.

We are aware that some horror books deal with sensitive topics and even prejudice, but we are all critical readers and such things are always acknowledged. Unlike the characters and themes in some of the books we read, we take the wellbeing of our community members very seriously and are welcoming to all. We strive to be a community for everyone, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, religion, and etc.
